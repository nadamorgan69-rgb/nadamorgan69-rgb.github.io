About me

Who Am I?

Engineering student who builds things that actually work — from schematic to soldering iron.

I'm Nada Morgan, a Mechatronics Engineering student on a dual-award programme between Ain Shams University and the University of East London, graduating in 2027.

Mechatronics appeals to me precisely because it refuses to let you stay in one lane. I started with circuits and kept pulling the thread — into firmware, then control theory, then full robot systems. Each project has pushed me to close the gap between simulation and something that actually moves.

On the hardware side, I design custom PCBs in EasyEDA — power boards with buck converters, charger ICs, and protection circuits — and simulate everything in LTspice before fabrication. On the firmware side, I write FreeRTOS tasks on ESP32, implement cascaded PID controllers, and build micro-ROS bridges that connect embedded MCUs to ROS 2 navigation stacks on a Raspberry Pi. When something doesn't behave, I reach for the multimeter and work through it methodically.

"I treat simulation as a starting point, not a finish line — the real test is always on the bench."

Delta Parallel Robot & 4-DOF Arm

Kinematics from scratch and hardware-in-the-loop control for two robot platforms: a delta robot and a 4-DOF serial arm.

  • Delta robot: derived FK/IK analytically in MATLAB, implemented cascaded PID on Joint 1 (DC motor with encoder feedback), measured pick-and-place repeatability via position standard deviation across runs.
  • 4-DOF arm: modelled in URDF/Xacro, planned collision-aware trajectories in MoveIt 2 and Gazebo, then bridged physical joints via micro-ROS on ESP32 for RViz-synchronised pick-and-place.
